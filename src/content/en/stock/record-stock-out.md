## How to Record Sales or Stock Out

Kelola.co offers a comprehensive stock management feature that helps you control inventory while tracking customer payments. Here's how to record sales (stock out):

### Step 1: Access Stock Out
1. **Log in to Kelola.co**.
2. **Select "Stock Out"** from the main dashboard.

### Step 2: Select Product and Quantity
3. **Choose the "Product"** you want to sell.
4. **Enter the quantity** being sold.

### Step 3: Select Customer
5. **Select "Customer"** to associate the sale with a customer. This helps track who made the purchase for future reference and payment tracking.

### Step 4: Set Payment Status
6. **Choose Payment Type** – Select one of the following options:
   - **Paid (Full)** – Select this if the customer has paid the full amount. The transaction will be marked as complete with no outstanding balance.
   - **Partial** – Select this if the customer made a partial payment. Enter the amount received, and the remaining balance will automatically appear in the [Receivables Report](../report/receivables-report).
   - **Unpaid** – Select this if no payment was received. The full amount will be recorded as receivable.

### Step 5: Complete Transaction Details
7. **Pick the "Date"** of the sales transaction.
8. **Add notes** if needed (optional).
9. **Click "Save"** and the system will:
   - Automatically update the stock quantity
   - Record the payment status
   - Update receivables if partial or unpaid

## Understanding Payment Options

### Full Payment
When you select **Paid (Full)**:
- Transaction is marked as completed
- No balance appears in Receivables Report
- Full amount recorded as revenue

### Partial Payment
When you select **Partial**:
- Enter the amount the customer has paid
- The remaining balance is automatically calculated
- Outstanding amount appears in the Receivables Report
- You can record additional payments later

### Unpaid
When you select **Unpaid**:
- Full transaction amount is recorded as receivable
- Entire amount appears in the Receivables Report
- Useful for credit sales or invoice-based transactions

## Managing Outstanding Payments

To view and manage unpaid or partially paid transactions:

1. Go to **Reports** → **Receivables Report**
2. See all customers with outstanding balances
3. Tap on a customer to view their unpaid transactions
4. Record additional payments as they come in

Learn more about managing receivables in the [Receivables Report guide](../report/receivables-report).

## Tips for Accurate Recording

- Always select the correct customer to enable payment tracking
- Record payments immediately to keep receivables accurate
- Use partial payment option when customers pay in installments
- Regularly check the Receivables Report to follow up on outstanding payments
