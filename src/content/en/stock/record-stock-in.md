## How to Record Incoming Stock (Stock In)

The **Stock In** feature allows you to record products entering your inventory, typically from supplier purchases. This increases your stock quantity and tracks your purchase costs accurately.

## Stock In Interface

Kelola.co provides a user-friendly two-panel interface for stock in:

- **Left Panel** – Product catalog to search and select products
- **Right Panel** – Selected products with quantities and buying prices

## How to Record Stock In

Follow these steps to record incoming stock:

### Step 1: Access Stock In
1. **Log in to Kelola.co** (Web: [web.kelola.co](https://web.kelola.co) or Mobile App)
2. From the main dashboard, select **"Stock In"**

### Step 2: Select Products
1. In the left panel, **search or browse** for the products you want to add
2. **Click on a product** to add it to your stock in list
3. The product will appear in the right panel
4. Repeat to add multiple products

### Step 3: Set Quantities
1. In the right panel, each product has a **quantity field**
2. Enter the number of units received for each product
3. The total quantity is automatically calculated at the bottom

### Step 4: Set Buying Prices (Optional)
For each product, you can set a custom buying price:
1. Click on the **buying price** displayed (with pencil icon)
2. Enter the purchase price for this transaction
3. Click **Save**

> **Note:** Custom buying prices require a subscription. This is useful when purchase prices vary between suppliers or orders.

### Step 5: Select Supplier (Optional)
1. At the top of the page, click on **"Choose Supplier"**
2. Search for an existing supplier or add a new one
3. This helps track which supplier each purchase came from

> **Tip:** Adding suppliers makes it easier to track purchase history and manage vendor relationships.

### Step 6: Set Transaction Date (Optional)
By default, the transaction is recorded with today's date. To change it:
1. Click on the **date field** at the top right
2. Select the desired date and time
3. Click outside to confirm

> **Note:** Setting past dates requires proper access permissions.

### Step 7: Add Notes (Optional)
1. At the bottom, find the **Notes** field
2. Add any relevant information (invoice number, delivery note, etc.)
3. Maximum 200 characters

### Step 8: Review and Save
1. Check the **total buying price** summary at the bottom
2. Verify all products and quantities are correct
3. Click **"Submit"** to save the transaction

Once saved:
- Stock quantities are **automatically increased**
- The transaction appears in your **Stock Report**
- Purchase costs are recorded for accounting purposes

## Understanding the Summary Bar

At the bottom of the Stock In page, you'll see:
- **Total Products** – Number of different products
- **Total Quantity** – Sum of all units
- **Total Buying Price** – Total cost of the purchase (requires read capital price permission)

## Tips for Accurate Stock In

### Use Barcode Scanner (Web)
For faster stock in on web:
1. Connect a USB barcode scanner
2. Click the **"Scan Barcode"** button
3. Scan product barcodes to quickly add items

Learn more: [How to Use Barcode Scanner](../features/barcode-scanner)

### Consistent Supplier Records
- Always link purchases to suppliers when possible
- This helps with vendor management and purchase history

### Custom Buying Prices
- Use custom buying prices when costs vary
- This ensures accurate cost of goods sold (COGS) calculations

### Backdated Entries
- Record stock in with past dates for missed transactions
- Useful when catching up on bookkeeping

## Managing Stock In History

To view or edit past stock in transactions:

1. Go to **History** from the main menu
2. Filter by **"Stock In"** transaction type
3. Tap any transaction to view details

> **Note:** Editing transactions is only available for Owner accounts.
